Final Project

Choice and Objective

Choosing the final project was straightforward for me. Since I am enthusiastic about space and 3D printing, I decided to design, build, and print a modular rocket. The core objective was to create a parametric rocket system featuring thrust vector control.

Research Process

I had already built smaller rockets at home and for school projects. I reviewed model-rocket build workflows and researched real launch systems through sources such as NASA and SpaceX. This helped me identify key design factors for stability, weight, and controllability.

Design Process

During the design phase, I focused on modularity and parametricity. I also explored a thrust vectoring concept that relies on controlled flexibility in printed plastic parts. Reducing weight while maintaining durability was another major constraint, so I iterated wall thicknesses and part geometry repeatedly.

Stable controlled flight was a central goal, which required integration of microcontrollers, gyroscopes, and servo motors.

Building Process

I fabricated the modules on a Bambu Lab 3D printer and designed them to twist-fit together. I also designed a dedicated lower assembly for the microcontroller, gyroscope, and battery system.

Lessons Learned

1. Persistence is essential.

My initial propulsion direction was not allowed for safety reasons, so I had to pivot quickly. Instead of stopping, I moved to an electronics-based stabilization strategy. That pivot became a successful Plan B.

2. Take calculated risks.

Collaboration introduced useful new ideas and implementation strengths that improved the overall result and complexity of the project.

3. Reliability takes time.

I underestimated development effort. The outer shell required multiple print iterations for proper fit, and the control stack (gyro + logic) required substantial testing and refinement.

Conclusion

The project met my goals. The next step is moving from the current validated system to full flight testing.
